1. What is an oscillator?
a) A device that converts AC to DC
b) A circuit that generates repetitive waveforms without external input signal
c) A device that amplifies signals
d) A circuit that filters noise
Answer: b

2. Which type of feedback is required for oscillation?
a) Negative feedback
b) No feedback
c) Positive feedback
d) Neutral feedback
Answer: c

3. What is the Barkhausen criterion for oscillation?
a) Loop gain must be less than 1
b) Loop gain must equal 1 and phase shift must be 0° or 360°
c) Loop gain must be greater than 2
d) Phase shift must be 180°
Answer: b

4. Which oscillator uses an LC tank circuit?
a) RC oscillator
b) Crystal oscillator
c) Hartley oscillator
d) Relaxation oscillator
Answer: c

5. What is the primary function of negative feedback in amplifiers?
a) Increase gain
b) Stabilize gain and reduce distortion
c) Generate oscillations
d) Increase output power
Answer: b

6. The frequency of oscillation in an RC oscillator depends on:
a) Resistance and capacitance values
b) Supply voltage only
c) Transistor type
d) Temperature only
Answer: a

7. Which oscillator is most stable and accurate?
a) RC oscillator
b) LC oscillator
c) Crystal oscillator
d) Relaxation oscillator
Answer: c

8. In a Colpitts oscillator, the feedback network consists of:
a) Two inductors and one capacitor
b) Two capacitors and one inductor
c) Three resistors
d) One capacitor and one resistor
Answer: b

9. What is the typical frequency range of a crystal oscillator?
a) 1 Hz to 100 Hz
b) 100 Hz to 1 kHz
c) 1 kHz to 200 MHz
d) Above 1 GHz only
Answer: c

10. Amplification factor (gain) is defined as:
a) Input voltage divided by output voltage
b) Output voltage divided by input voltage
c) Input current divided by output current
d) Power supply voltage divided by output voltage
Answer: b

11. Which component is essential in a Wien bridge oscillator?
a) Transformer
b) Crystal
c) RC network
d) LC tank circuit
Answer: c

12. What happens when loop gain exceeds unity in an oscillator?
a) Oscillations stop
b) Amplitude increases until limited by saturation
c) Frequency decreases
d) Circuit becomes stable
Answer: b

13. The phase shift oscillator produces a phase shift of:
a) 90 degrees
b) 180 degrees
c) 270 degrees
d) 360 degrees
Answer: d

14. Negative feedback in amplifiers results in:
a) Increased bandwidth and reduced gain
b) Decreased bandwidth and increased gain
c) No change in performance
d) Circuit instability
Answer: a

15. Which oscillator is commonly used in radio transmitters?
a) Relaxation oscillator
b) LC oscillator
c) RC oscillator
d) Multivibrator
Answer: b

16. Positive feedback increases the stability of an amplifier (True/False)
Answer: False

17. An oscillator requires an external input signal to generate output (True/False)
Answer: False

18. The quality factor (Q) of an LC circuit affects oscillator stability (True/False)
Answer: True

19. Negative feedback reduces the gain of an amplifier (True/False)
Answer: True

20. Crystal oscillators have better frequency stability than RC oscillators (True/False)
Answer: True
